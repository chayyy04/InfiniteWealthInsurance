FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The most common questions from our YouTube community — answered clearly and honestly.

What's the difference between AIA and PhilHealth? +
PhilHealth is a government benefit that covers a portion of hospitalization costs. AIA insurance is a private plan that fills the gap — providing lump sum cash for critical illness, death benefits, and daily hospital income. They complement each other; having both is ideal.
Can I get insured even if I have a pre-existing condition? +
It depends on the condition. AIA Philippines does underwriting on a case-by-case basis. Some conditions result in exclusions; others are accepted with loading. We'll be honest with you during the free consultation about your options.
How long before I can file a critical illness claim? +
Most AIA critical illness plans have a 90-day waiting period from the policy issue date. After that, a qualifying diagnosis triggers the claim process — typically resolved within 30 days of complete documents submission.
Is VUL a good investment? What are the risks? +
VUL gives you protection AND investment in one. The investment component is subject to market risk — your fund value can go up or down. It's best for long-term goals (10+ years). We teach you how to choose funds that match your risk appetite.
What if I can't pay my premium one month? +
AIA plans have a grace period (typically 31 days). After that, your policy may use accumulated cash value (for VUL plans) to keep coverage active. We proactively reach out to help you avoid lapse — your protection is too important to lose.
Can an OFW apply for AIA Philippines from abroad? +
Yes! We can assist OFWs with their AIA policy enrollment remotely. Some products require Filipino citizenship or residency, but many are available for OFWs. The premium can be set up for auto-debit or paid by a family member locally.
How is AIA different from Sunlife or Manulife? +
AIA Philippines (formerly Philam Life) is the market leader for over 70 years and has ₱257B+ in total assets. AIA Vitality is unique to AIA — no other Philippine insurer rewards you for being healthy. We're biased, but the data speaks for itself: AIA consistently pays claims and wins industry awards.
Is the AIA Critical Protect 100 really not affected by market risk? +
Correct. AIA Critical Protect 100 is a traditional whole life plan (not VUL), meaning your benefits are guaranteed and not subject to fund performance. It's one of the reasons it's our best-selling product — certainty matters when it's for your health.
What documents do I need to apply? +
Generally: valid government ID, TIN, latest photo, and health declaration form. Higher coverage amounts may require medical exams. We'll guide you through exactly what's needed for your chosen plan — no surprises.
Do you charge a consultation fee? +
Absolutely not. Our consultation, needs analysis, and presentation are 100% free. We are paid by AIA via commission only when you decide to proceed with a plan — and there's never any pressure to buy.
How do I access my AIA policy account? +
AIA Philippines has an online portal at aia.com.ph where you can view policy details, account values, payment history, and claim status. We also assist all our clients in setting up their online access and the AIA Vitality app.
